# Sabermetrics Glossary

## ERA
Earned Run Average estimates earned runs allowed per nine innings. It is useful context, but it depends on sequencing, defense, official scoring, and bullpen inheritance.

## FIP
Fielding Independent Pitching estimates pitcher run prevention from strikeouts, walks, hit batters, and home runs. It matters for prediction because those events are more pitcher-controlled than balls landing for hits.

## xFIP
Expected FIP replaces a pitcher's home run rate with a league-normalized home-run-per-fly-ball rate. It helps identify pitchers whose HR results may regress.

## SIERA
Skill-Interactive ERA estimates run prevention from strikeouts, walks, batted-ball tendencies, and interaction effects. It is a process metric, not just a result metric.

## WHIP
Walks plus Hits per Inning Pitched measures baserunners allowed. Lower WHIP usually means fewer traffic innings and lower blow-up risk.

## K%
Strikeout percentage is the share of batters faced or plate appearances ending in a strikeout. For pitchers, higher is better. For hitters, lower usually improves contact reliability.

## BB%
Walk percentage is the share of batters faced or plate appearances ending in a walk. Pitcher BB% measures command risk. Hitter BB% measures plate discipline and on-base skill.

## K-BB%
Strikeout rate minus walk rate is a strong pitcher quality signal because it combines bat-missing ability and command.

## HR/9
Home runs allowed per nine innings. High HR/9 raises total-runs and over risk, especially in hitter-friendly parks or wind blowing out.

## BABIP
Batting Average on Balls In Play. Extreme BABIP can signal defense, contact quality, speed, or luck. It often regresses, so avoid overreacting to small samples.

## wOBA
Weighted On-Base Average gives proper run-value weights to offensive events. It is better than batting average because a homer is worth more than a single.

## xwOBA
Expected wOBA estimates contact quality from exit velocity, launch angle, strikeouts, and walks. It helps separate process from noisy outcomes.

## wRC+
Weighted Runs Created Plus measures total offensive production adjusted for league and park, where 100 is league average. It is often better than OPS for offense evaluation because it is scaled to run creation and context-adjusted.

## OPS
On-base Plus Slugging is OBP plus SLG. It is simple and useful, but it double-counts some on-base value and is not park-adjusted by default.

## ISO
Isolated Power is SLG minus batting average. It isolates extra-base-hit power and helps identify home run or total-runs upside.

## Barrel%
Barrel rate is the share of batted balls hit with optimal exit velocity and launch angle. Higher barrel rate supports power, extra-base hits, and over potential.

## HardHit%
Hard-hit rate is the share of batted balls hit at 95 mph or harder. It is a stable contact-quality signal for offense and pitcher damage allowed.

## Park Factor
Park factor measures how a ballpark changes scoring or home run environment relative to league average. A run factor above 100 helps offense; below 100 suppresses runs.

## Pythagorean Win%
Pythagorean winning percentage estimates team strength from runs scored and runs allowed. It can reveal teams that are overperforming or underperforming their run differential.

## Log5
Log5 converts two team strength estimates into a matchup probability. It is useful as a neutral prior before adding starter, bullpen, lineup, and market context.

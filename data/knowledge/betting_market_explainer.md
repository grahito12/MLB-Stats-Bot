# Betting Market Explainer

## Moneyline
Moneyline is a bet on which team wins the game. Negative American odds show the favorite; positive odds show the underdog. Convert odds to implied probability before comparing to model probability.

## Run Line
Run line is baseball's spread market, usually around -1.5 or +1.5 runs. It is different from moneyline because the favorite must win by enough runs, while the underdog can often cover by losing close.

## Total
Total, or over-under, is a bet on combined runs by both teams. Common MLB totals include 6.5, 7.5, 8.5, 9.5, and 10.5.

## Market Implied Probability
Market implied probability converts odds into the chance implied by price. A model has edge only when its probability is higher than the market implied probability after accounting for uncertainty and vig.

## Market Movement
Opening line versus current line can show how the market reacted to lineups, weather, pitching changes, or sharp money. Movement alone is not a pick, but it changes whether value still exists.

## Model Total Versus Market Total
If model total is 9.2 and market total is 8.5, the model projects 0.7 runs above market. That supports an over lean, but confidence depends on the over probability, weather, park, pitchers, bullpen, lineup certainty, and price.
